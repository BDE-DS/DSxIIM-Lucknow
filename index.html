Training Overview

A Leadership Lens on Generative and Agentic AI

A two part, in-person AI leadership workshop for around 100 mid to senior level MBA professionals, designed from a management lens rather than a technical one. Session 1 runs 8 hours on Generative AI for decision making and communication. Session 2 runs 8 hours on Agentic AI, closing with a hands-on capstone build. Each session ends with a live team project review, giving participants a tested, applicable output to carry back to their organisations.

Session 1: Generative AI for Leadership and Decision Making

Module 01

AI and GenAI Literacy for Leaders and Decision Makers

Objective
Reset how senior professionals think about AI, moving from tool curiosity to a leadership lens on decision speed, judgment and team productivity.
Topics Covered
  • Difference between AI, Generative AI and Agentic AI in plain business terms
  • Why AI is a work redesign shift, not a software upgrade
  • How decision making changes when information access and analysis get faster
  • Live demo, AI reviewing a business case in minutes versus hours manually
  • Risks every leader should know, hallucination, bias and over reliance on AI outputs
Outcome
Participants speak a shared AI vocabulary and identify where faster decisions create real advantage in their own function.
Tools Covered
ChatGPTClaudeGemini
Module 02

Prompting and AI Powered Research for Strategic Decisions

Objective
Equip participants to use AI as a research and thinking partner for strategic problems, not just a search engine replacement.
Topics Covered
  • Structured prompting using a simple business framework, context, role, task, format
  • Asking better questions to get sharper, decision ready outputs
  • Multi step research for market, competitor and industry questions
  • Using NotebookLM to ground answers in verified company documents and reports
  • Spotting hallucinations and verifying AI generated claims before using them
Outcome
Participants run a structured AI research workflow independently and convert findings into a one page decision brief.
Tools Covered
ChatGPTClaudeNotebookLMPerplexity
Module 03

GenAI for Data, Reports and Leadership Insights

Objective
Show leaders how AI turns complex data, dashboards and lengthy reports into clear, decision ready insights.
Topics Covered
  • Converting spreadsheets, dashboards and operational reports into plain summaries
  • Highlighting trends, risks and performance gaps automatically using AI
  • Turning financial and operational data into board ready insight statements
  • Asking the right follow up questions to move from data to decision
  • Common mistakes leaders make when trusting AI generated numbers blindly
Outcome
Participants convert a real data set into a clear, leadership ready insight summary without manual analysis.
Tools Covered
ChatGPTClaudeGeminiExcel
Module 04

GenAI for Business Communication and Leadership Narratives

Objective
Help leaders convert data, reports and analysis into clear narratives for boards, teams and stakeholders using AI.
Topics Covered
  • Converting dashboards, spreadsheets and long reports into leadership ready summaries
  • Drafting board notes, town hall messages and stakeholder updates with AI
  • Structuring narratives using simple logic, situation, complication, recommendation
  • Creating quick visuals, charts and one pagers for presentations
  • Adjusting tone and depth of AI output for different audiences
Outcome
Participants draft a polished leadership document, slide outline or stakeholder update directly from raw data within the session.
Tools Covered
ChatGPTClaudeGammaCanva
Module 05

GenAI for Visual, Video and Audio Content in Business

Objective
Give leaders hands on exposure to generating images, video and audio content using AI, without a design or production team.
Topics Covered
  • Generating presentation visuals, social posts and marketing images using AI image tools
  • Creating short business videos, explainer clips and AI avatars for training and updates

Session 2: Agentic AI and Applied Leadership Projects

Module 07

Understanding Agentic AI and the Shift from Assistance to Autonomy

Objective
Build clarity on what AI agents are, how they differ from chatbots, and where autonomous workflows fit in a business.
Topics Covered
  • What makes an AI agent different from a chatbot or assistant
  • Real examples of agents handling research, scheduling, reporting and follow ups
  • Where agentic AI creates leverage, and where human judgment still leads
  • Risk and governance questions leaders must ask before deploying agents
  • Live demo, an agent completing a multi step business task end to end
Outcome
Participants identify two or three repeatable workflows in their team where an agent could remove manual effort.
Tools Covered
AntigravityChatGPTClaude
Module 08

No Code Agent Building for Business Workflows

Objective
Give leaders hands on experience building a working agent without writing code, using no code automation platforms.
Topics Covered
  • Mapping a business workflow into trigger, action and decision steps
  • Building a simple agent using n8n or Zapier style automation
  • Connecting AI models to everyday tools like email, sheets and forms
  • Testing, breaking and fixing the agent live in the room
  • Knowing when to automate versus when to keep a human in the loop
Outcome
Each participant builds and tests one working agent that performs a real task from their own role.
Tools Covered
n8nZapierChatGPTClaudeGoogle Sheets
Module 09

AI Driven Decision Frameworks for Leadership and Strategy

Objective
Translate AI capability into structured leadership decisions, covering prioritisation, risk and a practical AI adoption roadmap.
Topics Covered
  • Framework to identify quick wins versus long term AI bets
  • Evaluating AI use cases by impact, effort and risk
  • Building a 30 to 60 to 90 day AI adoption plan for a team
  • Common pitfalls, tool sprawl, weak governance and unclear ownership
  • Case discussion across BFSI, technology and FMCG and manufacturing examples
Outcome
Participants leave with a personalised AI adoption roadmap they can present to their own leadership.
Tools Covered
ChatGPTClaudeGemini

Multi Agent Systems and Where Agentic AI is Headed for Business

  • How multiple agents coordinate to complete a larger business process
  • Real world examples of multi agent systems in operations, finance and research
  • What roles and team structures look like as agents take on more work
  • Building an internal point of view on agentic AI adoption pace
Outcome
Participants articulate a forward looking view on agentic AI to bring back to their own leadership teams.

AntigravityChatGPTClaude

Apply the full two session learning to build and present a working agentic AI solution for a real problem.
